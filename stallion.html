The Black Stallion

I have lived eleven years among horses, and still the world feels too small for me everywhere except in their shadow.

Ordinary. Quiet. A heart that races — never quite falling below 120 — like a drum that forgot how to rest.

Shame has been my oldest companion… a low fog that clings to the skin.

I never quite fit in the places people gather. I only ever felt the air become breathable when a horse stood near.

Then, three or four years ago, the black stallion walked into my days.

He arrived wearing fear like a second coat.

I let go of perfection. I let go of force. I stood still. I breathed. I watched. I waited.

He began to believe.

He embraces me now… rests his head on my chest… in my lap… in my hands.

This horse chose me. Not because I became flawless. Because I became willing to be real.

Eleven years. One black stallion. A heart that finally learned how to rest.
