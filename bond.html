A Bond Beyond Words

I didn't find them. I want to be clear about that.

I didn't go looking. Didn't read a book about healing. Didn't follow someone's advice to try something new, get outside, it'll be good for you — all those clean, bright, well-meaning phrases that people hand to broken men like band-aids for amputations.

I didn't seek this out. I was found.

By something that doesn't speak. By something that has no interest in my performance, no use for my masks, no capacity to be impressed or disappointed or charmed — and that absence, that total, non-negotiable absence of everything I'd spent my entire life calibrating myself to provide —

That's what saved me.

—

Not the word. Not yet. The word comes later. First — the moment.

The first time I stood next to a horse.

Not rode. Not led. Not any of the verbs that imply action, direction, purpose. I just — stood.

In a field.

Next to a body so much larger than mine that the scale alone should have terrified me, and it didn't.

It didn't.

And the not-being-terrified was so foreign, so unrecognizable, that I almost mistook it for numbness.

Because I knew fear.

Fear was my operating system.

Fear was the electricity that powered every smile, every nod, every performance of adequacy I'd ever produced.

Fear was the thing I woke up to and fell asleep under and navigated through, every day.

I knew fear.

I was built on it.

And this animal — this enormous, breathing, warm animal standing three feet from me in a field that smelled like earth and rain and something older than language — didn't trigger it.

Not a flicker. Not a whisper. Not a single neural ping.

Instead — something in my chest released.

Not relaxed. Released.

Like a fist that had been clenched so long the fingers had fused… and something pried them open.

And underneath — just a heartbeat.

My heartbeat. Raw. Vulnerable. Alive.

And the horse heard it.

It turned its head. And it looked at me.

And the eyes — God. The eyes.

Not human. Not evaluating. Not judging.

Just still. Just present.

Just one message:

You are here.

And something inside me broke open.

And I stood there. And I breathed.

Deep. Fully. For the first time.

And the horse breathed with me.

And I thought:

This is what it feels like to be near something alive that wants nothing from you except your presence.

—

They teach me.

Not in words. Not in lessons. Not in the way humans teach.

They teach by being.

By existing in a state so grounded, so unperformative, so completely finished — that standing near them becomes a mirror.

And what's real… is enough.

They teach me patience.

Not waiting. Not suppression.

True patience — the absence of urgency.

The kind that lives only in the present moment.

I learn:

Love isn't rushed.

It isn't forced.

It isn't performed into existence.

The horse responds to one thing:

Consistency.

Not grand gestures.

Not intensity.

Just showing up.

Again. And again. And again.

Until one day…

The horse walks toward you.

—

And when it does…

The feeling isn't triumph.

It's grief.

Because you realize — this is what it was supposed to feel like all along.

Not forced. Not earned through performance.

Just given.

Freely.

And the grief is for every time you accepted less.

Every time you called a transaction love.

Every time you believed you had to become something to be allowed closeness.

But here —

You don't.

You just have to be.

—

I come back every day now.

Not because I'm healed.

But because something in me remembers.

And slowly — piece by piece — I am returning.

To myself.

To stillness.

To truth.

To presence.
