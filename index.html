The Horseman’s Journal

by Praveen

“a fragile human outside
and a horse inside”

The Black Stallion Who Whispered My Name in Hoofbeats

I have lived eleven years among horses, and still the world feels too small for me everywhere except in their shadow.

Ordinary. Quiet. A heart that races — never quite falling below 120 — like a drum that forgot how to rest.

Shame has been my oldest companion. A low fog that clings to the skin. Disgust followed — first from voices outside me, then echoing inside.

I never quite fit in the places people gather.

I only ever felt the air become breathable when a horse stood near.

For the first seven or eight years I trained as though perfection could save me.

The horses obeyed. Mostly.

But they never melted.

Something was always held back.

Then, the black stallion walked into my life.

He arrived wearing fear like a second coat.

He dragged me across the earth once — not from cruelty, but from terror.

And in that moment… something inside me broke open.

I was tired of shaping.

I was tired of force.

I was tired of proving.

So I stopped.

I stood still. I breathed. I waited.

Every day I came back the same — calm, steady, empty of agenda.

My hands softened. My voice lowered. My presence changed.

And slowly…

He began to believe.

Now he calls for me if I’m late.

He strikes the door. He lifts into motion when I arrive.

And sometimes…

he rests his head on mine.

On my chest.

In my hands.

In those moments…

everything that was broken becomes quiet.

I disappear. And something real remains.

Because this animal — this being who cannot lie —

chose me.

Not because I became perfect.

Because I became real.

Eleven years. One horse. A heart that finally learned how to rest.
